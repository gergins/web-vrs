# AI Ethics Framework (Accessibility Services)

## Purpose
Operational ethics framework for AI-assisted accessibility services where trust, autonomy, and safety are mandatory.

## Non-Negotiable Principles
1. Human primacy (AI assists, humans decide)
2. Accessibility equity
3. Transparency and explainability
4. Privacy and confidentiality
5. Non-replacement commitment
6. Fairness and bias mitigation
7. Safety and reliability
8. Accountability and governance

## Stakeholder Rights
### User rights
- human service access
- opt-out from AI assistance
- privacy protection
- understandable AI explanations

### Interpreter rights
- assistance-level control
- non-surveillance conditions
- fair evaluation context
- training access

## Mandatory Product Controls
- visible AI activity indicators
- consent management
- human override controls
- bias audit pipeline
- ethical risk review before release
- incident reporting channel

## Governance
- ethics board with community representation
- named accountability roles
- regular audits and policy updates

## Alignment and Limits
- aligns to responsible AI and accessibility-by-design practices
- not a substitute for market-specific legal advice
